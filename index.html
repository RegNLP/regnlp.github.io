Regulatory Natural Language Processing Workshop

The RegNLP 2025 Workshop will take place on either January 19th or 20th, 2025 (date to be confirmed) in conjunction with the COLING 2025 conference in Abu Dhabi, UAE.

About

Regulatory documents are crucial for governance, compliance, and legal frameworks across many sectors. However, their complexity, volume, and dynamism pose significant challenges that require specialized approaches in natural language processing for effective management.

This first workshop on Regulatory Natural Language Processing (RegNLP) aims to explore and advance NLP applications specifically tailored to the domain of regulatory documents.

Topics

We welcome submissions describing original work on regulatory data, as well as data with relevance to compliance and regulation, such as:

Applications of NLP to regulatory tasks including, but not limited to:

  • Compliance monitoring and management
  • Risk assessment and regulatory reporting
  • Interpretation and classification of regulatory changes
  • Summarization of regulatory documents for swift decision making
  • Creation of lexical and other data resources for the regulatory domain
  • Addressing bias and ensuring privacy in regulatory data processing

Experimental results using and adapting NLP methods for regulatory data including, but not limited to:

  • Information retrieval from complex regulatory texts
  • Anomaly detection in compliance monitoring
  • Clustering of regulatory requirements across domains
  • Knowledge base creation for regulation-specific information
  • Multimedia search in regulatory filings
  • Link analysis between various regulations and compliance documents
  • Entity recognition and disambiguation in regulatory texts
  • Training and using embeddings tailored to regulatory contexts
  • Parsing of complex legal and regulatory sentences
  • Dialogue and discourse analysis in compliance negotiations
  • Text summarization and generation for quick reference
  • Relation and event extraction from legislative activities
  • Anaphora resolution in lengthy regulatory documents
  • Question answering systems for regulatory queries
  • Query understanding in specific regulatory contexts
  • Combining text with structured data to enhance regulatory interpretations

Tasks:

  • Description of new regulatory tasks for NLP
  • Structured overviews of specific tasks with the goal of identifying new areas for research
  • Position papers presenting new visions, challenges, and changes to existing research practices

Resources:

  • Creation of curated and/or annotated data sets that can be publicly released and used by the community to advance the field

Demos:

  • Descriptions of systems which use NLP technologies for regulatory text processing

Industrial Research:

  • Industrial applications that address specific regulatory compliance needs
  • Papers describing research on proprietary data within regulatory contexts

Interdisciplinary position papers:

  • Analyses relating to the role NLP can play in the regulatory field
  • Critical reflections on the benefits and challenges of Large Language Models (LLMs) from a regulatory perspective
  • Critical reflections on the legality and ethics of data collection and processing practices in compliance settings

Submission

We welcome original (unpublished) research papers in the following categories:

  • Long Papers: Up to 8 pages, excluding references
  • Short Papers: Up to 4 pages, excluding references

Note: Appendices and acknowledgements do not count towards the page limit and should adhere to the formatting guidelines detailed below.
